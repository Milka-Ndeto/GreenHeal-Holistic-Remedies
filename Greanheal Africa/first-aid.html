First Aid Basics

Knowing how to respond in an emergency situation can save lives. Here are some basic first aid steps for common medical emergencies.

1. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

CPR is a life-saving technique used when someone's breathing or heartbeat has stopped. Here's how to perform it:

  • Check if the person is breathing. If not, start CPR immediately.
  • Place the person on their back and kneel beside their chest.
  • Place your hands on the center of their chest and push hard and fast (at least 2 inches deep and 100-120 compressions per minute).
  • After every 30 chest compressions, give 2 rescue breaths.
  • Repeat until help arrives or the person starts breathing.

2. Treating Burns

Burns can range from mild to severe. For any burn, follow these steps:

  • Cool the burn with running cold water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover the burn with a clean, non-stick bandage or cloth.
  • Do not pop blisters. If the burn is severe, seek immediate medical help.

3. Stopping Bleeding

If someone is bleeding heavily, follow these steps to stop the bleeding:

  • Apply firm pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Keep the person calm and encourage them to stay still.
  • If bleeding doesn't stop, apply more pressure or elevate the injured area if possible.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if the bleeding is uncontrollable.

4. Choking

Choking occurs when an object blocks the airways. Follow these steps:

  • Ask the person to cough if they can.
  • If they cannot breathe or cough, perform the Heimlich maneuver: Stand behind them, place your arms around their waist, make a fist, and apply quick inward and upward thrusts.
  • If the object is dislodged, check if the person is breathing. If not, start CPR.
